About

Food deserts are areas in which citizens have limited access to nutritious food and affordable groceries. Rather than supermarkets, or grocery stores that stock fresh produce, these areas have corner stores or gas stations with limited shelf space. Deserts leave many local communities at much greater risks of weight-related conditions including obesity, diabetes, heart disease, and high blood pressure.

The USDA characterizes a food desert by:

  • A poverty rate of 20%
  • 33% of the population residing more than 1 mile from the nearest grocery store in urban areas
  • 33% of the population residing more than 10 miles from the nearest grocery store in rural areas
